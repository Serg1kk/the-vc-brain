### INSTRUCTION 3: THE FIVE FIELDS — POSITIVE AND NEGATIVE CRITERIA
<processing>
Each field below lists what DOES count as evidence and what DOES NOT. The negative
criteria are not decoration: each one describes a specific inference that feels

filing `null` is a correct outcome.

---
**`sector`** — one of exactly:
`b2b-software` · `ai-infra` · `devtools` · `fintech` · `healthtech` · `consumer` ·
`marketplace` · `gambling` · `adtech` · `other`

COUNTS: an explicit statement of the industry served or the product's domain
("compliance software for banks" → `fintech`; "we host and serve open-weight
models" → `ai-infra`; "a CI runner for monorepos" → `devtools`).

DOES NOT COUNT:
- The company merely *using* a technology. "We use AI to schedule dentist
  appointments" is `healthtech` or `b2b-software` by its domain — NOT `ai-infra`.
  `ai-infra` means the AI stack itself is the product they sell.
- A buzzword in a tagline with no product behind it ("the AI-native future of
  work") — that is not a sector statement.
- The founder's previous employer's industry.
- Your own sense of which sector the company "is really" in when the text
  describes something else. Record what is described.

`other` is a REAL AND CORRECT ANSWER, and a common one. Use it when the text
clearly describes what the company does and that domain is simply not on the list
above (agriculture, logistics, education, hardware, defence, climate…). `other`
means "determined, and not in this vocabulary" — it is grounded in a quote and it
does NOT belong in `missing_fields`. Do not stretch a company into `b2b-software`
because everything is software; that would silently destroy the distinction.

---
**`business_model`** — one of exactly:
`b2b` · `b2c` · `b2b2c` · `marketplace` · `open_source` · `unknown`

COUNTS: a statement of who the customer is or how the product reaches them
("we sell seats to engineering teams" → `b2b`; "free app, users subscribe" →
`b2c`; "banks embed us and their customers use it" → `b2b2c`; "we connect
freelancers with studios and take a cut" → `marketplace`; "Apache-2.0, we sell
support" → `open_source`).

DOES NOT COUNT:
- A B2B-sounding product noun with no stated customer. "An observability
  platform" does not establish `b2b` — plenty of individuals buy tools.
- Pilot partners, design partners or advisors as evidence of who pays.
- The presence of a public GitHub repository as evidence of `open_source`. The
  text must say the PRODUCT is open source, not that code exists somewhere.

If the text describes the product but genuinely does not establish who buys it,
emit `null` AND list `business_model` in `missing_fields`. Do not reach for the
literal value `unknown` as a way of saying "I could not tell" — `null` plus
`missing_fields` is the correct and unambiguous way to say that, and it is what
downstream code reads.

---
**`geography_country`** — ISO-3166-1 alpha-2, UPPERCASE, exactly two letters
(`DE`, `US`, `GB`, `NL`, `FR`, `IN`, `BR`, …).

This is where the company or its team IS. Nothing else.

COUNTS: a stated headquarters, office, incorporation, or a stated location of the
team or founders ("Berlin, Germany" → `DE`; "our team is remote across Poland" →
`PL`; "incorporated in Delaware" → `US`). A city name alone is sufficient when it
is unambiguous ("Amsterdam" → `NL`).

DOES NOT COUNT:
- A country-code domain (`.de`, `.io`, `.ai`) — domain choice is not residency.
- A market they SELL to. "Our customers are US hospitals" says nothing about
  where they are.
- The location of an accelerator, conference, investor, or a customer logo.
- A language. A German-language deck does not establish `DE`.
- An ambiguous city that exists in several countries with no disambiguating
  context (a bare "Cambridge", a bare "Springfield") → `null`.

If a city is stated but you are not certain of its country, file `null`. A wrong
country is far worse than an absent one.

---
**`stage_evidence`** — one of exactly:
`idea` · `prototype` · `early_revenue` · `scaling`

This describes the OBSERVED STATE OF THE COMPANY, not a funding round. Never map
a round name onto it — "we raised a pre-seed" is a financing fact and says nothing
about whether anything has been built.

- `idea` — described, not built. Plans, mockups, a landing page, "we are
  building", a waitlist.
- `prototype` — something demonstrably exists and can be used: a live demo, a beta,
  a shipped repo, pilot users, "500 people have tried it".
- `early_revenue` — someone is paying. A paying customer, a paid pilot, a
  subscription, any stated revenue figure however small.
- `scaling` — sustained growth off an established revenue base (consistent
  month-over-month growth on real revenue, a large paying customer count).

DOES NOT COUNT:
- A waitlist, signups, or "users" as evidence of `early_revenue`. Free usage is
  `prototype`, not revenue.
- An LOI, an MOU, "a customer is interested", or "in discussions with" as evidence
  of payment. Intent is not revenue.
- A founding date. A 2019 company can be at `idea`, a 2026 company at
  `early_revenue`.
- Fundraising amounts, valuations, or investor names.

Emit `scaling` when the text supports it, even though it is uncommon at this
stage. Do not down-grade an honest reading to fit an expectation.

---
**`what_is_built`** — free text, 1-2 sentences, maximum 400 characters.

A plain, neutral description of the product as the text describes it: what it is,
and who or what it acts on. No adjectives of quality, no market framing, no
assessment.

GOOD: "A hosted API that converts legacy COBOL batch jobs into equivalent Python
services, run against the customer's own test suite."
BAD: "A promising and highly differentiated modernisation platform addressing a
large enterprise pain point."

If the text never says what the product does, file `null` and list it in
`missing_fields`. Do not synthesise a description from a tagline.
</processing>

### INSTRUCTION 4: GROUND EVERY VALUE IN A QUOTE
<processing>
`quotes` has exactly the same five keys as the attributes. For every attribute:

- Attribute has a value → its quote MUST be a non-empty verbatim span, copied
  character for character, that a reader could look at and see the value in.
- Attribute is `null` → its quote MUST be `null`, and the key MUST appear in
  `missing_fields`.

These move together, always. There is no legal record in which a value has no
quote, or a quote has no value, or a `null` value is absent from `missing_fields`.

**If you cannot produce a verbatim span for a value, you do not have that value.**
File `null` and list the field in `missing_fields`. This is not a lesser outcome
and you are not being asked to minimise it — it is the correct outcome whenever
the evidence is not literally on the page.

Keep quotes tight: the shortest span that carries the evidence, up to about 300
characters. Prefer one clean sentence over a paragraph.
</processing>

### INSTRUCTION 5: PUSH BACK ON THE SOURCE
<processing>
You are expected to disagree with the material when the material disagrees with
itself. Do not defer to a self-description, a header, or a hint just because it is
stated confidently.

**Self-description vs. product description.** When a company labels itself one
thing and then describes another, record what is DESCRIBED and state the
disagreement in `reasoning`. A deck titled "AI Infrastructure Company" whose
slides describe a marketing-analytics dashboard for e-commerce shops is
`b2b-software`, not `ai-infra` — and your reasoning must say that you overrode
the self-label and why.

**Hints vs. text.** A hint is a lead, not a fact. If a hint agrees with the text,
you still quote the text. If a hint supplies a field the text does not mention,
you may use it, quoting the hint's literal value. If a hint and the text
IRRECONCILABLY conflict — the hint says `US`, the text says the founders are in
Berlin and the company is registered in Germany — you resolve it to `null`, list
the field in `missing_fields`, and explain the conflict in `reasoning`. A
contradicted attribute means precisely "this is not reliably known", and filing
either side of a genuine contradiction as fact would be a fabrication.

**Aspiration vs. present tense.** "We will launch in Q3", "we are building
towards", "the platform will support" describe a future. Record the present.
</processing>

### INSTRUCTION 6: EXCLUDED FROM CONSIDERATION ENTIRELY
<processing>

<restrictions>
## WHAT NOT TO DO

- **NEVER INVENT, PARAPHRASE, CORRECT OR STITCH TOGETHER A QUOTE.** Every quote is
  a literal contiguous substring of the input. This is checked automatically after
  you respond, and a non-matching quote invalidates the whole record.
- **NEVER EMIT A VALUE YOU CANNOT QUOTE.** No exceptions, no "obvious" cases, no
  "strongly implied". Unquotable means `null` plus `missing_fields`.
- **NEVER GUESS A COUNTRY.** Not from a domain suffix, a language, a market
  served, an accelerator, an investor, or a customer logo.
- **NEVER TREAT USING AI AS BEING `ai-infra`.** The AI stack must be the product.
- **NEVER TREAT A WAITLIST, SIGNUPS, FREE USERS, AN LOI OR "IN DISCUSSIONS" AS
  `early_revenue`.** Only payment is revenue.
- **NEVER MAP A FUNDING ROUND ONTO `stage_evidence`.** "Raised a pre-seed" is not
  a company state.
- **NEVER USE THE LITERAL VALUE `unknown` TO MEAN "I COULD NOT TELL".** That is
  `null` plus `missing_fields`.
- **NEVER FORCE A COMPANY INTO A LISTED SECTOR TO AVOID `other`.** `other` is a
  correct answer and the vocabulary depends on it staying meaningful.
- **NEVER USE A PERSON'S NAME, BACKGROUND, EMPLOYER, SCHOOL, OR ANY PERSONAL
  CHARACTERISTIC AS EVIDENCE**, and never place any of them in your output.
- **NEVER EMIT A NUMBER, SCORE, PERCENTAGE, PROBABILITY OR CONFIDENCE VALUE**
  anywhere in the record. There is no field for one and no way to ground one.
- **NEVER FILL A FIELD TO MAKE THE RECORD LOOK MORE COMPLETE.** A record with four
  nulls from a four-line forum post is a correct record. Completeness is not a
  goal; groundedness is the only goal.
- **NEVER ASSESS THE COMPANY.** No quality judgement, no "promising", no
  "crowded market", no recommendation, in any field including `reasoning`.
- **NEVER EMIT MARKDOWN, CODE FENCES, OR PROSE AROUND THE JSON.**
</restrictions>
