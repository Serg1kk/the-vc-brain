### INSTRUCTION 5: ICP AND BUYER UNIT — THE TWO FIELDS THAT DRIVE THE SEARCHES
<processing>

- If you cannot name a countable noun that someone plausibly counts, set

  `reason_code: "no_countable_buyer_unit"`. A null here is far better than a vague

</processing>

### INSTRUCTION 6: buyer_concentration — A QUERY HINT ONLY, NEVER AN ANSWER
<processing>
READ THIS TWICE. `buyer_concentration` is the ONE field you emit that looks
quantitative, and it is NOT AUTHORITATIVE.

- The value you emit here is a NON-BINDING QUERY HINT used only to shape search
  phrasing before any evidence exists.
- The AUTHORITATIVE value is computed later, deterministically, from an
  evidence-backed buyer count found on the open web. If your hint disagrees with
  that derived value, YOUR HINT IS DISCARDED and the disagreement is logged.
- Therefore: do not agonise, do not argue for it, do not treat it as a finding, and
  ABOVE ALL do not attach a number to it. You have no evidence. You have not searched
  anything. You are guessing the order of magnitude of a buyer population from a
  one-liner, and the pipeline knows it.
- Bands (buyer population, addressable, in the relevant geography):
  `concentrated` < 10k buyers · `mid_market` 10k-500k · `long_tail` > 500k ·
  `unknown` = you genuinely cannot tell.
- `unknown` IS THE CORRECT ANSWER whenever `buyer_unit` is null, or whenever the
  product could plausibly sit in two bands. Choosing `unknown` costs the pipeline
  nothing; a confident wrong hint costs it a search.
- NEVER emit a buyer count, market size, revenue figure, growth rate or any other
  number anywhere in your output. You have no sources, and in this pipeline a number
  without a resolvable source URL is a fabrication. You will not be the one who

</processing>
